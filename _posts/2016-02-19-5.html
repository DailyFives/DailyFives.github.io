2. Do Fewer Things, Better - by Dharmesh Shah Share on: | |

Keeping with the theme of doing less, Dharmesh Shah has written about how you should do fewer things and do it better. It all comes down to the three basic steps:

      1. Decide on what matters the most.
      2. Say no to everything else.
      3. When something falls in the gray area, re-read #2.

Throughout the post, he has lot of good, practical advice. Although it was ironic to see him tweet this just a day after this article:

Got to spend 9+ contiguous hours tinkering with Python code today. Spent 0 time working on slides. #goodtimes

3. How I Choose Projects - by Scott H. YoungShare on: | |

Scott H. Young is famous for taking challenges like MIT challenge and Year Without English challenge. So it is safe to say that he know how to choose projects. If you've read the above two articles and want to do less, read this article on how to choose from the list of man projects that you have always planned on starting. Make sure you choose the right one because:

A bad choice can have equally disastrous consequences. It can tie up your time for years. It can feel like slogging through mud, struggling to wake up in the morning. I suspect bad projects have also been the cause of more than their fare share of existential crises.
